Cinnamon Buns

Description

Boldly alleging to be the best cinnamon buns in the world, these ooey-gooey delights are sure to please. They are big, fluffy and oh so moreish. The recipe includes instructions to make them overnight and freezing them for later!

Ingredients

For the Dough

  • 3/4 cup warm milk
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons quick rise or instant yeast
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 egg + 1 yolk at room tempurature
  • 1/4 cup butter, melted
  • 3 cups bread flour + more for dusting
  • 3/4 teaspoon salt

For the Filling

  • 2/3 cup dark brown sugar
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 cup butter, softened

For the Frosting

  • 4 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 3 tablespoons butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

Directions

  1. Warm milk to around 110 degrees F. Transfer warm milk to a bowl and sprinkle yeast on top. Add in sugar, egg, egg yolk and melted butter. Mix well with wooden spoon until dough begins to form.
  2. Knead dough for 8-10 minutes either by hand or with an electric mixer with a dough hook. Dough should for into a nice, slightly sticky ball. If too sticky, add more bread flour.
  3. Transfer dough to a well-oiled bowl, cover with plastic wrap and a warm towel. Allow dough to rise for 1 hour to 1 1/2 hours or until doubled in size.
  4. After dough has doubled in size, transfer to a well floured surface and roll into a 14x9 inch rectangle. Spread softened butter over rectangle, leaving a 1/4 inch margin at the far side of the dough.
  5. In a small bowl, mix together brown sugar and cinnamon. Sprinkle mixture over the buttered dough, then gently rub into the butter.
  6. Tightly roll dough up, starting from the shorter edge. Place roll seam side down, sealing edges as much as possible. Cut 1 inch of either side of the roll to ensure filling goes all the way to the edges.
  7. Using a serrated knife, cut roll into 1 inch sections. Place sections into a greased, lined 9x9 inch pan and leave to rise for 30-45 minutes.
  8. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until edges are just golden brown. Let cool for 5-10 minutes before frosting.
  9. To make the frosting, combine cream cheese, butter, sugar and vanilla extract. Mix until light and fluffy.
